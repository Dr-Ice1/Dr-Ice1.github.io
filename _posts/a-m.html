Clearly, we can see the most trusted Islamic source (in this case Aisha herself) reporting the age of Aisha being 6 years old when she was married off to Mohammed and 9 years old when he consumated the marriage. However, many Muslims will try to justify this event. We will look at each of these arguments and refute them.

Here are the most common arguments made:

  • Aisha was mature and not a child.
  • It was culturally accepted at that time.
  • The Quran says one has to start the age of puberty to marry.

While these are only some of the arguments, they are the ones I personally heard many times. So now, let us address these points.

Refutation

Aisha was mature and not a child.

This claim is usually made with the assumption that during that time period in that culture, the starting age was counted from puberty and not from birth. This assumption suggests that Aisha may have been considered mature and at an age deemed acceptable for marriage even by present-day social standards. However, this is not the case when we look at Islamic laws and examine the sources. By law, dolls and puppets are forbidden from the people since they are seen as idols, and idolatry is considered a grave sin. Only when a child has not started puberty are dolls permissible and seen as children's toys.2 We can read about this in the Sahih sources:

Narrated 'Aisha: I used to play with the dolls in the presence of the Prophet, and my girl friends also used to play with me. When Allah's Messenger (ﷺ) used to enter (my dwelling place) they used to hide themselves, but the Prophet would call them to join and play with me. (The playing with the dolls and similar images is forbidden, but it was allowed for 'Aisha at that time, as she was a little girl, not yet reached the age of puberty.) (Fath-ul-Bari page 143, Vol.13)
Sahih al-Bukhari 6130 — Chapter 81: To be cheerful with the people, Book 78: Good Manners and Form (Al-Adab)
https://sunnah.com/bukhari:6130

Now we know who can play with dolls, we look at what the sources say about Aisha, in Sahih Muslim and Sunan an-Nasa'i we read:

'A'isha (Allah be pleased with her) reported that Allah's Apostle (ﷺ) married her when she was seven years old, and he was taken to his house as a bride when she was nine, and her dolls were with her; and when he (the Holy Prophet) died she was eighteen years old.
Sahih Muslim 1422c — Chapter 10: It is permissible for a father to arrange the marriage of a young virgin, Book 16: The Book of Marriage
https://sunnah.com/muslim:1422c

It was narrated that 'Aishah said: "The Messenger of Allah married me when I was six, and consummated the marriage with me when I was nine, and I used to play with dolls."
Sunan an-Nasa'i 3378 — Chapter 78: Consummation Of Marriage With A Girl Of Nine, Book 26: The Book of Marriage — Grade: Sahih (Darussalam)
https://sunnah.com/nasai:3378

We clearly read how Aisha used to play with dolls and have them with her, meaning she could only be before the age of puberty when the marriage was consumated.

The Quran says one has to start the age of puberty to marry.

This claim is usually in reference to Quran 4:6 which only addresses orphans. But when we look at Quran 65:4 which is a Surah about divorce, we read something contradictory to this claim. The verse talks about the waiting period after marriage for three categories of women: 1) those who are too old to menstruate, 2) those who have not menstruated, and 3) those who are pregnant.
