Newton

  • N1: A body remains at rest, or in motion at a constant speed in a straight line, unless it is acted upon by a force.
  • N2: At any instant of time, the net force on a body is equal to the body's acceleration multiplied by its mass or, equivalently, the rate at which the body's momentum is changing with time.
  • N3: If two bodies exert forces on each other, these forces have the same magnitude but opposite directions.
To sum up: $$ \begin{align*} F&=ma=\frac{\text{d}\vec{p}}{\text{d}t}\;\text{or}\;\text{d}\vec{p}=\vec{F}\text{d}t\\ F_{12}&=-F_{21} \end{align*} $$ Furthermore we have a concept called impuls (NL. stoot). This is best explained with an example:

$$ \begin{align*} \underbrace{\int_{t_0}^{t_0+\Delta t} F \, \text{d}t}_{\text{impuls}} = \int_{t_0}^{t_0+\Delta t} dp = p_{\text{end}}-p_{\text{begin}} \end{align*} $$

Ratio of Mass $$ \begin{align*} \begin{cases} F=m_1a_1\\ F=m_2a_2 \end{cases}\implies \frac{m_1}{m_2}=\frac{a_2}{a_1} \end{align*} $$ Momentum
$$ \begin{align*} F_{12}=-F_{21}&\Longleftrightarrow m_1a_1=-m_2a_2\\ \int m_1a_1+m_2a_2 \, \text{d}t = \int \, \text{d}t&\implies\color{red}{}\boxed{\color{black}m_1v_1+m_2v_2=const.} \end{align*} $$

Work and Force

Definition $$ \begin{align*} dW&:= \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}\\ W_{12}&=\int_1^2 \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r} \end{align*} $$ Acquiring expression relating Work and Kinetic Energy $$ \begin{align*} W_{12}&=\int_1^2 \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int \vec{F}\cdot\frac{d\vec{r}}{dt}dt=\int\vec{F}\cdot\vec{v}dt=\int m\frac{d\vec{v}}{dt}\cdot\vec{v}dt\\ &=m \int \vec{v} \cdot d\vec{v} = m \int \frac{1}{2} d\vec{v}^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \vec{v}^2 \bigg|_1^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \vec{v}_2^2 - \frac{1}{2} m \vec{v}_1^2\\ &=\Delta E_{\text{kin}} \end{align*} $$
